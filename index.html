Selected Works

Featured Project

I / 2025

Liacouras Design School

Educational Facility — North Philadelphia

A public magnet high school for young designers, conceived as a transparent, hands-on learning environment rooted in making and collaboration. Organized around open circulation and shared workspaces, the building promotes visibility between studios, classrooms, and communal areas. An exposed mass-timber structure, abundant daylight, and flexible learning spaces support creative exploration while establishing a warm, legible civic presence.

Educational Mass Timber Civic 4 Floors Philadelphia
Exterior Perspective — Entry Plaza

II / 2024

Riad Apollo

Residential Mixed-Use — Philadelphia

A mixed-use apartment building and creative hub designed to support minority and immigrant communities in Philadelphia through housing, work, and shared cultural space. Integrates residential units with studios, collaborative workspaces, and community-oriented amenities across seven floors, with an open courtyard at its core.

Mixed-Use Residential Community 7 Floors Philadelphia
Isometric Model View

III / 2024

H.A.L.O

Holistic Architecture for Living & Outreach — Mixed-Use Civic

A multiuse civic and wellness center rooted in North Philadelphia's social, cultural, and health landscape. Integrates community outreach, educational spaces, clinical care, and a pharmacy. A sweeping internal circulation spine wraps a C-shaped interior void, transforming vertical movement into a daylight-filled promenade.

Civic Wellness Mixed-Use Healthcare
Exploded Axonometric — Program Levels

IV / 2023

HomeFront

Children's Health Center — Community Clinic

Reframes pediatric healthcare through the scale and familiarity of residential architecture. A raised, semi-circular skylight forms a daylight-filled circulation spine. Exam rooms line this central path with individualized "homefront" façades evoking the rhythm of a neighborhood street, organized into age-specific quadrants.

Healthcare Pediatric Community
Exterior Perspective — Entry

V / 2023

The Green House

Single-Family Residential — Tokyo, 2049

Explores the future of dwelling through adaptive spatial systems responding to environmental, social, and technological change. Designed as a prototype for living in 2049. Located in Tokyo, integrating flexible living spaces, layered circulation, and indoor–outdoor transitions. Aligned with the Tokyo Zero Emissions Plan.

Residential Sustainable Tokyo Speculative
Exterior Perspective — Garden Passage

VI / 2023

Post-Carbon Fishtown

Large-Scale Urban Framework — Master Plan

A phased revitalization master plan envisioning Fishtown over 15 and 100-year horizons. Emphasizes bikes, pedestrians, and public transportation. Streets progressively recede car infrastructure, returning space to nature, people, and transit. By 100 years, cars are fully eliminated from the neighborhood.

Urban Design Master Plan Sustainability Philadelphia
Master Plan Overview

VII / 2022

Elfreth Alley House

Single-Family Residential

A residential design situated within one of America's oldest continuously inhabited streets. The project navigates the tension between historic context and contemporary living, responding to the intimate scale and material character of Elfreth's Alley.

Residential Historic Context Philadelphia
Elfreth Alley House

VIII / 2022

Cliff House

Case Study

An analytical case study exploring architectural response to extreme topography. Examines how structure, enclosure, and program are mediated by a dramatic clifftop site, investigating the relationship between building and landscape at the limits of conventional construction.

About

Architecture graduate currently completing a Master of Architecture at Temple University (2026), building on a Bachelor of Science in Architecture earned in 2024.

Work focuses on thoughtful space planning, designing with intention, and the relationship between structure, enclosure, and daily inhabitation. Values pragmatic, buildable architecture that responds directly to context, users, and material constraints.

Primary interests span residential, commercial, and mixed-use design, with a particular focus on how architecture can serve communities — from civic wellness centers to housing that supports creative livelihoods.

In parallel with academic work, served as Junior Architect at Blackwell & Associates — gaining hands-on experience across residential, educational, and commercial projects through construction documentation, site surveys, and code research.
